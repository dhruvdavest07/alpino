1 Intr oduction  and Description  of the Pr oject 
 
1.1 Intr oduction   
Alpino  Health  Foods  is an Indian  brand founded  in 2016  in Surat under the name  Esterella Overseas.   
Their objective  was to cater to the growing  demand  for protein among  consumers.  They  have  created a clean 
brand that sells products  like peanut  butter , protein powder , protein bars, and rolled  oats.  
 
Their idea  is to keep  the nutrition in the food  intact by  minimal  processing  and little to no  preservatives  and 
artificial flavors.  They  also featured  on  Shark  Tank Season  1 in 2021. They  are currently present in 15+ 
countries.  They  have  also received  recognition  from  PM  Narendra  Modi,  the Great Khali,  and have  currently 
partnered  with  Shilpa  Shetty  as their brand ambassador .  
 
1.2 Statement  of purpose   
The purpose  of our capstone  project is to provide  the company  with  a framework  for opening  100 franchise  
stores  nationwide.  It includes  the following  objectives  -  
1. Establish  franchise  business  model  and SOPs  to franchise  partners. 
2. Develop  a clean label cafe  menu  with  a suitable  pricing structure.  
3. Develop  an expansion  plan  on  a city-by-city  basis, taking  the 15  most  recommended   cities, and the plan  of 
market-entry . 
4. Prepare  marketing  launch  kits and other support  materials,  an investor  / franchise  pitch deck  and ROI  model.  
5. Construct  a tech-enabled  support  system  and create  a conclusive  action  blueprint on  100 cafe  expansion.  
 
1.3 Pr oblem  Statement   
Lack  of a clean-label  food  brand that serves  the growing  demand  of consumers  for healthy and protein-rich  
food.  
 
1.4 Description  of pr oblem  statement

health-conscious  consumers.

1.5 Theor etical Framework   
 
The current research  falls into the theoretical framework  of the franchising  theory , the consumer  behavior  
theory  and operations  management   principles. The theory  of franchising  has stressed  that standardization,  
control systems  and distinct distribution  of functions  between  franchisors  and franchisees  is essential to achieve  
scalable  and consistent  growth.  These  are especially  applicable  in food  and beverage  related businesses  where  
uniformity  in product  quality, service provision  and brand experience  is paramount  in customer  confidence.  
 
The theory  of consumer  behavior  is the basis of the analysis  of the increased  demand  of protein-rich  and 
clean-label  food  choices.  Lifestyle choice, and health  and nutrition awareness  largely  determines  the food  
decisions  made  by  urban and semi-urban  consumers.  These  trends  in behavior  characterize  the growing  
popularity  of protein cafes and support  the grandiose  of the Alpino  Health  Foods  to spread  the cafe  format  in 
Tier 1 and Tier 2 cities. 
 
This  project is further informed  by  operations  management   concepts  like process  standardization,  cost control,  
capacity  utilization and inventory  management.  Franchised  cafes require efficient operations  since differences  
in staff skills, menu  complexity , and the supply  chain practices would  directly  influence  profitability and 
scalability.  
 
The systems  that are enablers  include technology-based   systems,  including  POS,  inventory  management,  and 
CRM  platforms,  which  offer visibility, control,  and data-driven  decision-making  within  various outlets. 
 
These  theories  are combined  and incorporated  in a conceptual  model  within  the scope of this project where  
consumer  awareness  encourages  consumer  demand,  consumer  demand  dictates  menu  design  and pricing,  menu

 1.6 Limitations  of the Study  
 
This  research  paper has some  drawbacks  that must  be listed. The main  study undertaken  for the project is 
limited to a few cafes and locations,  which  might  not fully capture consumer  behavior  and operations  across  all 
parts of India.  In addition, the financial and operational  data  collected at the cafe  visit is estimated  and 
management-oriented   as opposed  to audited financial statements,  and this may  undermine  the validity  of the 
cost and profitability analysis. 
 
The project is also oriented  toward  the conceptual  design  of franchise  models,  SOP  frameworks,  and technology  
systems  rather  than  their active  implementation.  The conclusions,  therefore,  will be directed  to strategic 
planning  and decision  making,  and not as an operations  guide.  In spite of these shortcomings,  the study offers 
rich information  sufficient for consideration  during  academic  review  and strategic recommendations.   
 
1.7 Significance  of Pr oject for Pr ofessional  Practice  
 
The capstone  project is of great importance  to professional  practice  in business  strategy , franchising,  and food  
and beverage  management.  It shows  how  theory  can be used  into practical models  with  help  of field research  
and systematic  analysis. The project offers a scalability methodology  for health-based  cafe  brands  and handles  
the universal  constraints  of uneven  operations,  employee  reliance, and cost management.  
 
To the entrepreneurs  and practitioners  in the industry , the project will provide  a template  on  how  to expand  the 
franchise  to include menu  development,  pricing,  technology  facilitation, and market  analysis  city by  city. It is an 
example  of interdisciplinary  problem-solving  in the form  of a combination  of consumer  behavior , operations  
management,  and strategic planning  for students  and academic  professionals.

1.8 Definition  of Term  
 
Clean-Label  Food:  Food  items that are processed  with  little or no  food  preservatives,  flavorings,  or other food  
additives.  
 
Protein Cafe: A cafe  based establishment  whose  idea  is to provide  protein-based  food  and beverages  in line with  
health  and fitness objectives.  
 
Franchise  Model:  It is a type  of business  where  franchisor  gives out license of its brand systems  and processes  
to the franchisee.  
 
Standard  Operating  Procedures  (SOPs):  This  is written-down  policies  that guarantee  that food  is prepared,  
served, staff trained,  and inventory  managed  in a consistent  manner . 
 
Average  Order  Value  (AOV):  This  is the average  dollars a customer  spends  on  each  transaction.  
 
Unit  Economics:  This  is a financial performance  analysis  done  at the outlet level. 
 
POS  (Point  of sale system):  This  is a digital system  that can be utilized  in billing, inventory  tracking, and sales 
reporting.  
 
CRM  (Customer  Relationship  Management):   Solutions  that are employed  to control customer  information,  
loyalty programs,  and repeat-based  engagement.

2 Literatur e Review  
The health  based food  and drink industry  has been  increasingly  on  the upward  trend today because  of the 
surging  level of lifestyle diseases  and heightened  health  consciousness  among  the consumers  since the covid.  
Customers  have  begun  shifting  to high-calorie  foods to those that are nutritious. According  to Mordor  
Intelligence  the existing  Indian  protein market  is estimated  to be USD  1.52  billion and is expected  to increase  at 
a CAGR   of 6.5 till the year  2030. The main  reasons  behind  this accelerated  growth  are the increasing  
urbanisation,  the exposure  of the people  to worldly  food  trends  due to social  media  and the augmented  capacity  
of the people  to spend.  
The current literature on  the topic of the health-based  F&B  sector  equally points  to the fact that in spite of the 
high  consumption  of ener gy-dense  food  by  people, the Indian  society has a serious protein deficit. According  to 
a study done  by  Reuters  and other nutritional surveys,  nearly  73  percent of Indians are deficient in protein.  Due  
to this imbalance,  there has been  the appearance  of protein-rich  food, functional  foods and vegan  protein as 
viable  options.  
According  to a study conducted  by  CEEW  , the current protein consumption  of Indians amounts  to 
approximately  56  grams  per day, but half of this is merely  the intake  of low-quality  amino  acid-containing  
cereals like wheat,  maida,  and suji, which  are poorly  digestible. This  high  cereal  intake  as a source  of protein 
exceeds  the recommended   32  percent of cereals.  Other high-quality  sources  of proteins  are not taken or 
consumed  due to lack  of awareness,  non-availability  among  rural people, or under-provision  of these 
high-quality  protein sources  within  government  food  programmes   like the Public  Distribution  System.  
Indian  diets are very  reliant on  cooking  oils and wheat  or rice. In India,  pulses  are not being consumed  
according  to the required  levels  and are contributing  about 11 percent of the protein consumption  with  the 
required  levels  being 19  percent,  thus  forming  an enormous  nutritional disproportion.  The other point of 
concern  is the salt content in Indian  diets. It contains  approximately  11 grams  of salt daily, which  is twice the 
amount  advised  by  the WHO  of 5 grams  of salt per day. In order to deal  with  these issues, there isa  need  to 
ensure  that protein-rich  food  offerings are available to Indian  citizens  at affordable  prices, conveniently , and in a 
way  that is acceptable  to all. 
Furthermore,  while conducting  research  on  food  service trends, I noticed that health-oriented  cafes are gaining  
popularity  and have  become  a strong  niche in the food  and beverage  industry . In an article published  in The 
Times  of India,  it is stated  that the increasing  culture of gyms  has prompted  the existing  food  brands, food

The Indians consume  very  little protein compared  to the 48  grams  per day recommended   by  the Indian  Council

solutions.Nevertheless,  it also highlights  the role of affordability and accessibility, a concern  raised  in previous

3 Methodology   
 
To ensure  the project is grounded  in actual  market  behavior  rather  than  guesswork,  we  chose a practical,  
field-based  research  methodology . We have  started by  visiting  established  healthy cafes in Ahmedabad   to 
understand  their operations.  We communicated   with  their managers  and attempted  to understand  their pricing 
scheme,  menus,  customer  awareness,  footfall, etc. 
 
Having  identified  the supplier  position, we  are now  successfully  conducting  a survey  of health-conscious  folks 
to find out what  consumers  want. We shall concentrate  on  learning  more  about their food  habits, protein 
consciousness,  price consciousness,  their favorite  time  to eat, etc. This  assists in the demand-side  expectations  
of the potential customers.  
 
The findings  from  cafe  visits and consumer  surveys  will be merged and evaluated  to create  a scaled  menu,  
pricing structure,  and customer  experience  at Alpino  Protein Cafe. Such  results will be subsequently  utilized  to 
develop  a viable  franchise  structure,  business  principles, and an urban-scale  expansion  strategy  to grow  100 
cafes in India.  
 
Besides  field visits and consumer  research, we  will also use information  provided  by  the company , including  
sales figures,  average  order values, and details on  the software  systems  used  at Alpino.  This  will be analyzed  in 
order to better understand  the real consumer  base, preferences,  and purchasing  behavior . This  will help  us keep  
the final recommendations   we  make  for this project rooted  in the realities on  the ground  and the project's overall 
objectives.

4 Cities  Recommendation:    
As  per our analysis, the following  cities provide  best opportunities  for Alpino  Protein Cafe  to expand  their 
franchise  outlets. 
  
1. Delhi:  
A large portion of its population  comprises  young  professionals,  student communities,  and fitness enthusiasts  
who  are health-conscious  and frequent  gym-goers.  Such  people  are usually short of time  and skills to cook  their 
own  meals,  and thus  they  trust cafes and restaurants  that can deliver quality post-workout  meals  in a convenient  
and inexpensive  manner . According  to an industry  indicator, the post-workout  meals  are a new  category  that is 
gaining  23%  market  share.  This  shows  that it accounts  for almost  a quarter of total restaurant  visits in Delhi,  
with  customers  being proactive  in ordering  foods rich in protein and carbohydrates.  
 
2. Chandigarh:  
It is regarded  as a food  hub in North  India.  Even  more  Generation  Zs  and millennials  are going to cafes.  The 
recent  boom  in health-focused  cafes and restaurants  shows  that healthy food  is in high  demand  among  urban 
dwellers.  The city’s inclusion  in Swiggy’ s ‘High Protein Category’  program  highlights  that there is increased  
demand  for protein-based  food  within  the city. 
 
3. Hyderabad-  
India's sixth-largest food  services  market  is projected  to grow  by  12  percent per year . It is composed  of a 
better-organized  vegetarian  sector . There  is high  QSR  penetration  due to urbanization,  a professional  youth 
population,  and increased  disposable  income.  
 
4. Bengaluru:   
Bengaluru  is the most  health-conscious  city in India,  with  the largest concentration  of start-ups and tech  
professionals,  and a strong  culture of gyms/  yoga  classes.  A large percentage  of the population  adheres  to the 
organized  fitness programs  and is interested  in high-protein,  functional  food, particularly  after physical  activity. 
The lack  of time  among  the working  population  makes  cafes that offer easy  access  without  reducing  the 
nutritional value more  reliable. Despite  the competition,  the massive  size of the addressable  market  and 
Bengaluru's  high  acceptance  rates make  the city a strategic flagship  city in terms of brand visibility and scale.

commercial  estates. A protein cafe  that serves  clean-label,  affordable  food  without  sacrificing taste can easily

7.  ROI  Analysis  
-Assumptions   
A ROI  Model  is prepared  to assess  the feasibility of opening  a protein-based  café  in Surat and Ahmedabad.   The 
model  is built using certain assumptions-  a mid-size  800 sq. ft QSR  with  a seating capacity  of around  20-30  
people, and nine  staff working  at the café. The café  will be primarily  operated  with  the Company-Owned    
Company-Operated    (COCO)  Model.  This  means  that all the profits will be retained  by  the company  itself, and 
similarly, all the investment,  hiring  and training  of employees  and managing  operations  will be done  by  the 
Alpino  Protein Café. 
To estimate  revenue,  a bottom-up  approach  is followed,  by  identifying  the numbers  of orders  expected  to be 
received  per day. Since revenue  is mainly  driven  by  the Average  Order  Value, an AOV  of ₹ 300.00  is assumed  
in the base  case. An  initial capex  of ₹36.7 lakhs is assumed  which  covers  kitchen equipment,  furniture and 
fixtures, interiors, POS  system  costs, and security  deposit of around  3-6 months  of rent. It is assumed  that the 
useful  life of these assets  or the costs of these assets  will be amortised  over  10  years using the straight-line 
depreciation  method.  Furthermore,  the costs have  been  segregated  into fixed and variable  costs, with  variable  
costs including  the raw materials,  packaging,  and the storage costs and fixed costs include rent, fixed marketing,  
electricity, and internet  and software  char ges. 
Following  that, free cash  flows are calculated  by  adjusting  the operating  profit for depreciation,  changes  in 
working  capital and initial capex  costs. IRR  is derived  using free cash  flows to understand  the overall return  
generated  by  the investment.  
-Analysis  of Results 
Overall,  an IRR  of 29.20%  in Surat indicates that the café  would  be highly  profitable  and scalable. However , 
the returns are sensitive to the orders  received  and the Average  Order  Value. 
  
 
13 
----------------Page (12) Break----------------
 
 
At  an AOV  of ₹250, the returns become  negative  which  indicates that the revenue  is not enough  to cover the 
costs, and the project is not economically  viable. However , even  a modest  increase  in the AOV  to ₹300, 
increases  IRR  significantly  to around  25%,  and further to around  65%  when  AOV  is ₹350. This  highlights  how  
important  it is to make  effective pricing decisions  so that profits are achieved.  This  significant rise in IRR,  given 
that AOV  is changed  by  a proportionately  smaller amount  might  be because  of a strong  operating  leverage.  
Fixed costs remain  constant  while increasing  revenue  boosts  profitability.  
Another  key driver  is the customer  footfall. Similar to AOV , a slight increase  or decrease  in no  of orders  can 
dramatically  change  IRR.  At  an IRR  of around  25%,  the breakeven  for the café  happens  at around  4.6 years 
which  seems  reasonable  in these types of cafes in the F&B  industry . 
Thus, this sensitivity analysis  highlights  that the company  needs to have  a strong  brand identity  in order to keep  
having  a large number  of orders  and hence  be profitable  and also the pricing must  be competitive  so that it 
attracts customers,  boosts  revenue  and returns,  and not generate  losses.
